1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This playbook is our end-to-end guide to organizing a one-day conference on “AI-Led Quality Engineering — The Proof of Value” in Delhi NCR. It covers every phase from initial planning through post-event follow-up, calibrated for a 200–300 attendee event within a limited budget and a 3–4 month preparation window.
The conference targets QA engineers, test leads, SDETs, engineering managers, and CTOs who are navigating the shift toward AI-augmented testing. The goal is to establish your organization as a thought leader in this space while creating genuine community value.
Key Success Metrics
Attendance: 300+ registered, 200+ actual attendees (assume 65–70% show rate)
Speaker Quality: 2 keynotes from recognized industry voices + 8–10 community talks
Engagement: 80%+ attendees rate the event 4/5 or higher in post-event survey
Pipeline: 50+ qualified leads for consulting conversations
Content: 10+ pieces of post-event content (blogs, videos, social clips)

4. VENUE SELECTION — DELHI NCR
For a 200–300 person tech conference, prioritize venues that offer a main auditorium/hall (theatre-style seating for 250–300), at least one breakout room, a foyer/lobby for networking and sponsor booths, on-site catering, and strong Wi-Fi.
Recommended Venue Types
Hotel Conference Centers
Tech Park Auditoriums
Co-Working/Event Spaces
University Auditoriums
Venue Checklist
Capacity: Confirm theatre-style seating for your target number + 10% buffer
AV: Built-in projector, screen, PA system, wireless mics (at least 3)
Internet: Dedicated Wi-Fi for 300+ concurrent devices (test this during visit)
Power: Adequate power outlets for speaker laptops, AV gear, and charging stations
Parking: Sufficient parking or metro accessibility (Gurgaon Rapid Metro, Delhi Metro lines)
Catering: On-site kitchen or permission for external caterer
Accessibility: Wheelchair access, ramps, accessible restrooms
Branding: Permission to put up standees, banners, sponsor displays in foyer
5. THEME DEEP-DIVE & AGENDA DESIGN
The theme “AI-Led Quality Engineering — The Proof of Value” deliberately focuses on proving measurable business outcomes from AI in testing, not just tool demos. Structure the agenda around four thematic pillars:
Thematic Pillars
Pillar 1 — The Strategic Case: Why AI in QE now? What’s the business case? ROI frameworks, executive buy-in, and maturity models.
Pillar 2 — The Technical Foundation: AI-assisted test generation, self-healing locators, visual testing, LLM-powered test design. Hands-on proof does not slide.
Pillar 3 — The Human Element: Reskilling QA teams, changing team structures, the evolving role of the tester, career paths in an AI-first world.
Pillar 4 — The Proof of Value: Case studies with real metrics. Before/after numbers. Failures and what they taught. This is the heart of the conference.
Sample Agenda
6. KEYNOTE SPEAKER STRATEGY
We need 2–3 keynotes who bring credibility, draw attendees, and set the intellectual tone. For a first event, focus on accessible, community-connected leaders rather than unattainable celebrity speakers.
Speaker Profile Targets
Keynote 1 — The Industry Voice: VP/Director of Engineering or QA at a major product company (e.g., Flipkart, Freshworks, Zoho, Thoughtworks, Infosys). Someone who can speak to AI in QE from real organizational experience.
Keynote 2 — The Technical Visionary: Founder/CTO of an AI testing startup or a well-known open-source contributor in the testing space. Think founders of AI-native testing tools or prolific conference speakers.
Closing Keynote — The Provocateur: An analyst, author, or consultant who can deliver a forward-looking, opinionated take. Shorter slot (20 min), high-energy.
How to Approach Speakers
Start outreach in Week 1. Keynotes take 4–8 weeks to confirm.
Personalized email explaining the theme, audience profile, and why they specifically fit.
Offer: travel + accommodation covered, prominent billing, video recording rights shared.
Leverage LinkedIn — most India tech leaders are highly active there.
Ask confirmed speakers to recommend others — this is your best pipeline.
Have 2–3 backup names for each keynote slot.

Anti-Pitch Policy
Make it crystal clear in the CFP that vendor pitches will be rejected. Language to use: “We welcome talks from tool creators IF the talk focuses on engineering challenges and real outcomes, not product features. Pure product demos belong in the sponsor booth, not on stage.”
8. MARKETING & REGISTRATION STRATEGY
Registration Platform
Use Konfhub or Townscript — both are India-friendly, support UPI/Razorpay, and generate attendee badges. Konfhub is especially popular with Indian tech events and has a free tier for community events.
Sample Pricing Strategy
Marketing Channels
LinkedIn (Primary): This is your #1 channel for the QE audience. Post 3–4 times/week: speaker announcements, CFP reminders, behind-the-scenes, polls. Personal posts from organizer accounts outperform company pages 5x.
Testing Communities: Ministry of Testing, QA communities on Slack/Discord, Testing Tribes India, STeP-IN Forum. Don’t just drop links — engage genuinely.
Twitter/X: Use event hashtag consistently (e.g., #AIQEConf2026 or #ProofOfValueQE). Tag speakers in announcements.
Email Campaigns: Build a list from registration, CFP, and partner sharing. Send 6–8 emails total: launch, speaker reveals, CFP close reminder, early bird ending, final agenda, last chance.
WhatsApp Groups: Underrated in India. If you have access to QA/testing WhatsApp groups, use them judiciously (not spammy).
Partner Amplification: Ask sponsors, speakers, and community partners to share with their networks. Provide them ready-made posts and graphics.

10. DAY-OF-EVENT LOGISTICS
Core Team Roles (Assign by Week 10)
Event Day Checklist
T-minus 2 hours: Team arrives. AV check, slide deck test for every speaker, Wi-Fi speed test, signage up, registration desk ready.
T-minus 1 hour: Caterer setup confirmed. Sponsor booths ready. Photography/videography team briefed.
T-minus 30 min: Keynote speakers mic-checked. MC/host briefed. Registration opens.
During event: Stage manager keeps strict time (flash cards at 5 min and 1 min remaining). Social media team captures every session.
Post last session: Group photo. Feedback QR codes at every table. Open networking facilitated (not just “people standing around”).
AV & Technology Setup
Primary projector + backup (or venue’s built-in). Test resolution with actual slide decks.
3 wireless mics: 1 lapel for speaker, 1 handheld for Q&A, 1 backup.
Speaker timer visible on stage (use a tablet or dedicated app — Stagetimer.io is free).
Confidence monitor (screen facing the speaker) if budget allows.
Live streaming: Use a simple setup — OBS + YouTube Live/LinkedIn Live. One dedicated person for this.
Recording: Record all talks for post-event content. Even a phone on a tripod works if budget is tight.
Wi-Fi: Test with 50+ device load beforehand. Have a fallback mobile hotspot for speakers.
11. ATTENDEE EXPERIENCE DESIGN
Great conferences are remembered for how they made people feel, not just what they taught. Design intentional experiences beyond just “talk → break → talk.”
Registration & Welcome
Pre-print name badges (not handwritten). Include name, company, and role. Color-code by type: speaker, sponsor, attendee, volunteer.
Welcome kit: badge, lanyard, printed agenda card, pen, and one high-quality swag item (branded notebook or tote bag).
A dedicated “first-timers” table at registration for people attending their first conference — pair them with a volunteer buddy.
Networking Facilitation
Ice-breaker activity during breakfast: e.g., “Find someone who…” bingo card with QE-themed prompts.
Designated “networking zones” in the foyer with standing tables and conversation starter cards.
A shared WhatsApp or Telegram group created 2 days before the event for attendees to connect.
During lunch, assign random table topics: “AI in Mobile Testing,” “Career Growth in QE,” etc.
Engagement Touchpoints
Live polling during panels using Slido or Mentimeter (free tiers available).
A physical or digital “Idea Wall” where attendees post their biggest QE challenge for others to respond to.
Best question award — incentivizes audience participation during Q&A.
Photo booth area with conference-branded backdrop for social media sharing.
12. POST-EVENT FOLLOW-UP
The 48 hours after the event are as important as the event itself. This is where you convert attendees into community members, leads, and advocates.
Within 24 Hours
Send thank-you email to all attendees with: feedback survey link (Google Forms), event photos album (Google Photos/Drive), promise of talk recordings “coming in 2 weeks.”
Post highlight reel on LinkedIn — photos, 2–3 key quotes (with permission), attendance numbers.
Send personalized thank-you notes to speakers and sponsors.
Within 1 Week
Publish a blog post: “5 Key Takeaways from AI-Led QE Conference 2026.”
Share individual speaker highlight clips on social media (30–60 second cuts).
Send sponsor deliverables: attendee list (with consent), post-event report with metrics.
Within 2–3 Weeks
Upload full talk recordings to YouTube (with speaker permission).
Publish detailed session recaps as individual blog posts — SEO goldmine.
Send a “Stay connected” email with links to community channels, upcoming meetups, and a hint about the next event.
Lead Generation for Your Consulting Business
This conference is ultimately a brand and pipeline investment. Capture value through:
A free “AI Readiness Assessment” offered at your booth or in the closing remarks.
Follow-up emails segmented by role: CTOs get strategic content, SDETs get technical guides.
Invite engaged attendees to a post-event “AI QE Office Hours” webinar (free, value-driven).
Use the talk recordings and blog content as ongoing lead magnets on your website.
